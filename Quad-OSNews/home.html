Quad-OS News


Beta has been released!


In the Beta version of Quad-OS, the settings and local storage have been removed, however, two new editions were included:

  • A battery level reader
  • Scribble

One of the more important features that were added to Quad-OS is Scribble. Scribble is a text editing platform which is a work-in-progress. Despite the fact that scribble is a work-in-progress, it is in a stable version and can be used. The developer of Scribble, Amar M (who is also the developer of Quad-OS), is planning to add local storage to it to save a few text documents written by the user.

-July 19, 2016


Box has been added to Quad-OS


Today, on June 14, 2016, a special personal assistant has been addded to Quad-OS. According to the Developers, one of them who is the creator of Quad-OS, Box is meant to be like a blank notebook. When you open it for the first time, its "notebook" is blank, except for a few questions and responses. Now what happens is that while you ask it questions or have a conversation with it, if it does not know how to answer what you said, then it will ask you to give the answer and provide Key Words. What this does is it creates a notebook for Box which is like its database. Additionally, even when you close your browser, or shut down your computer and turn it back on, it will save what is in its "notebook" for the next time you use it. Box can also do Math and tell you the time. As said by the main developer of Box, Kevin C, there are a few more updates to come, one of them to be a major update.
